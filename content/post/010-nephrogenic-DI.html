Off-target effects

However, this model is over-simplistic. One additional factor at play is that thiazide diuretics can exert an “off-target” inhibition of carbonic anhydrase in the proximal tubule. This will inhibit sodium reabsorption through the sodium-bicarbonate exchanger and thus shift sodium re-absorption to the water-impermeable distal tubule, where sodium reabsorption favours water excretion.
