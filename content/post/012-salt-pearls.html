Summary

Salt reduction lowers blood pressure and prevents CVD. Ditto K supplementation. More effective at a population than an individual level. Contribution from kidney, vasculature, CNS, immune system - but primacy of pressure natriuresis and the RAAS. Salt preference because salt activates dopaminergic mesolimbic reward pathways.

Clinical pearls

The units of salt

100 mmol Na = 2.3 g Na = 6g NaCl 100 mmol K = 3.9 g K

…and link to nephromaths page for meds etc.

Recommended daily Na = less than 5 g NaCl = 2 g Na = 86 mmol = 4 packets of crisps or 500 ml 0.9% NaCl or 1 tsp table salt.
Na mainly in bread, pizza, sandwiches, processed meats, soups. Big Mac = 1000 mg; packet of crisps = 500 mg.

Recommended daily K = more than 3.5 g K = 90 mmmol = 7 bananas K content in foods: baked potato (skin on) = 1000 mg; banana or glass or orange juice = 500 mg. K mainly in in fruit, veg, seafood, dairy.

Others

  • 50 mM Na reduction = 2.0 - 2.5 mmHg drop in ABP; mean is 4 mmHg (c.f. 5 mmHg for a drug)… = 10% RRR in CVS disease
  • …but RCTs of short duration and perhaps hard to sustain over weeks (effects smaller over 3 - 6 months+) - what does this mean in terms of Big Macs? - plant-based, fresh food better
  • gradient higher if older, non-white, hypertensive (and probably also DM - animal studies); salt-sensitivity of ABP in CKD and also slower to re-equilibrate to changes in Na load
  • K supplementation interventions = 3 mmHg drop in ABP - a thiazide-like effect?
  • salt-substitutes reduced CVS disease
  • sodium and calcium transport correlated in the PCT - so sodium reduction should increase PCT Na uptake and PCT Ca uptake (lowering UCa)
  • can retrain / reset thresholds over weeks / months
  • eating a healthy diet requires knowledge, time and money - a strong argument for population-level intervention
  • amiloride might help to limit salt intake (read the study - complex!)
